Phillip Seymour Hoffman

"Perhaps the most ambitious and widely admired American actor of his generation"

First Oscar wins for Hoffman (pictured left) for Capote and Reese Witherspoon at the 78th Academy Awards

Philip Seymour Hoffman (July 23, 1967 – February 2, 2014) was an American actor, director, and producer. Best known for his distinctive supporting and character roles—typically lowlifes, eccentrics, bullies and misfits—he acted in many films, including leading roles, from the early 1990s until his death in 2014.

In a 2006 interview with 60 Minutes, Hoffman revealed he had engaged in drug and alcohol abuse during his time at New York University, saying he had used "anything I could get my hands on. I liked it all."[145] Following his graduation in 1989, he entered a drug rehabilitation program at age 22, and remained sober for 23 years. However, he relapsed in 2013, and admitted himself to drug rehabilitation for about ten days in May of that year.

Filmography and Timeline:

  • July 23, 1967 - Philip Seymour Hoffman is born in Fairport, a suburb of Rochester, N.Y.

  • 1976 - Hoffman's parents divorce. In early high school, Hoffman gives up playing several sports when he suffers an injury.

  • 1991 - He stars in an indie film, Triple Bogey on a Par Five Hole, credited as "Phil Hoffman." The next year he appears in his first role in a major release, My New Gun

  • 1997 - His breakthrough role in Boogie Nights earns him a reputation as a rising young actor

  • 1999 - Meets girlfriend Mimi O'Donnell, a costume designer, while working on the play In Arabia We'd All Be Kings.

  • 2000 - Receives nomination for his first Tony Award as best actor in a play for a revival of True West.

  • 2005 - Hoffman landed the lead role in ‘Capote.’ For this biographical film about Truman Capote, Hoffman took four months to prepare for the role. He received an ‘Oscar’ for his extraordinary acting.

  • 2006 - Wins the best-actor Oscar for the title role in Capote, for which he garners 23 awards total. He would be nominated again his supporting roles in Charlie Wilson's War, Doubt and The Master.

  • 2012 - Hoffman got an opportunity to play the lead role in the Broadway revival of ‘Death of a Salesman.’ He received critical acclaim for his acting skills and also a ‘Tony Award’ nomination.

  • 2013 - Announces he has checked into a drug treatment center for heroin abuse. The Hunger Games: Catching Fire is released in November, in which he plays Plutarch Heavensbee.

  • Feb. 2, 2014 - Hoffman dies at age 46 in his Manhattan apartment
